Continuous Improvement Engineer

Paula Barth Campani

I am a Structural Engineer (RPEQ) and Project Manager (PMP) who is passionate about the amusement devices industry and innovative technologies. My career journey in the amusement devices industry began in Canada, where I worked at Dynamic Structures, utilizing cutting-edge technology to design, test, and manufacture new rides. In 2017, I migrated to Australia and soon joined Village Roadshow Theme Parks (VRTP) as the Sea World Engineering Manager. In this role, I oversaw the Park Technical Services department and led a team of over 50 staff, all of whom were male.

 

In 2021, I experienced the incredible surprise of welcoming my twins, Alex and Julia. After a year of maternity leave, I returned to work as a Continuous Improvement Engineer at VRTP. In this capacity, I have been managing multiple exciting and innovative projects across all VRTP parks, including Wet and Wild, Movie World, and Sea World. I am genuinely thrilled to be driving innovation, efficiency, and change within the theme park industry.

The remarkable career opportunities provided by the Gold Coast industry, combined with the opportunity to enjoy an early surf before work, form a combination of factors that lead me to describe my life as a dream come true.

 

Career & Key Achievements

Graduating as a Structural Engineer from UFRGS, Brazil in 2011, Paula started her engineering career in the Oil and Gas industry in Mexico—an amazing opportunity to consolidate her practical engineering skills and work with new industry technologies.

In 2015, she moved to Canada to study Project Management and assumed the role of Structural Engineer/Assistant Project Manager at Dynamic Structures, where she managed the test programs for two Research and Development roller coaster projects, further extending her management skills and passion for the industry.

Joining VRTP in April 2019, Paula's main contributions include:

  • Successful opening of the New Atlantis precinct, a multi-million-dollar project at Sea World with three new rides, including the iconic wooden coaster Leviathan. Her involvement encompassed various stages, from design and construction to commissioning, and preparation of maintenance and training documentation.
  • Mentoring the engineering team across the company, facilitating meetings to improve communication, delivering continuous professional development, and enhancing change management processes.
  • Attainment of the "License to Operate a Major Theme Park." Paula played a pivotal role as the technical services representative in creating the "Safety Case" document. Her responsibilities encompassed creating and implementing safety processes, not only focused on meeting compliance requirements, but also creating efficient and maintainable workflows.

Current Projects

Paula's leadership shines through her management of transformative projects, including the implementation of Mobaro and Ignition systems, in place to transform safety, efficiency, sustainability, and data-driven decision-making in the company:

  • Mobaro: Paula is managing the integration of Mobaro, a computerized maintenance management system. This system streamlines safety, maintenance, and operational processes, optimizing workflows and ensuring timely compliance. Transitioning from paper-based methods, Mobaro's mobile management solutions enable checklist completion via mobile devices, eliminating 16,000 sheets of paper annually and drastically reducing processing time. Mobaro's integration further enhances the robust compliance training process Paula has put in place, by ensuring that tasks can only be performed by qualified staff.

  • Ignition: Paula's management extends to the implementation of Ignition, a versatile software facilitating data acquisition applications, logic control, and customized reporting dashboards. This empowers the company with tailor-made solutions such as:

    - Leviathan Timber Inspection: Utilizing interactive drawings, Ignition improves the efficiency, accuracy, and quality assurance assessment of over 20,000 timber members inspected annually.

    - Flood mitigation: By enabling real-time equipment monitoring and control, Ignition ensures prompt detection of equipment defects and initiates proactive flood measures. This strategic implementation not only promotes operational reliability but also safeguarding vital assets.

Vision

Paula envisions her career future marked by innovative projects aimed at enhancing safety, efficiency, sustainability, and guest experiences. Her main future projects plan and expected outcomes are:

Maintenance Trend Analysis Tool: Paula intends to develop a tailored software solution that acquires data from the ride Programmable Logic Controllers (PLCs) and from maintenance defect/fault logs. The analysis of acquired data facilitates early failure detection, proactive maintenance and optimize maintenance scheduling. This initiative promises to heighten ride safety, reliability, and availability for park visitors.

Energy Consumption Monitoring and Automation: Paula aims to revolutionize energy management within the park by implementing automated systems for monitoring and controlling components such as air conditioners, heat pumps, chillers, and filtration systems. By optimizing energy usage, this project aligns with VRTP sustainability goals while contributing to cost reduction and resource efficiency.

Wizard of Oz Precinct: Paula is part of the "Wizard of Oz" Movie World projects, featuring captivating attractions like a suspended roller coaster and a boomerang racer ride. Designed for high capacity and with inclusive height and age restrictions, these attractions are set to elevate the park experience for families.
